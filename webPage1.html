Human Chromosome 11

You can search on gene identifier, protein product names, Genbank accession, or chromosomal location.



Human chromosome 11 spans about 134.5 million base pairs and represents between 4 and 4.5 percent of the total DNA in cells. It is one of the most gene- and disease-rich chromosomes in the human genome. This website provides a great tool for playing around with it. Here you can view and search its gene's DNA and amino acid sequences of the coding or non-coding regions. You can also view the codon usage frequency within the coding regions and to identify the sticky-end restriction enzyme sites.
